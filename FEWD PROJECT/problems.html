Challenges and Barriers

Gender Discrimination


Women often face discrimination in various areas including education, employment, and political participation. This can lead to unequal opportunities and limitations on their advancement.

Sexual harassment and violence


Women are often subjected to sexual harassment and violence, both in public and private spaces. This can have a significant impact on their mental and physical well-being, and restrict their freedom of movement.

Unequal pay


Despite progress in some countries, women continue to earn less than men for doing the same work. This perpetuates the gender pay gap and contributes to women's financial insecurity.

Lack of representation in leadership positions


Women are underrepresented in leadership positions in business, government, and other areas of society. This can limit their ability to make decisions that affect their lives and the lives of others.

Stereotyping and gender roles


Stereotyping and gender roles can limit women's opportunities and contribute to discrimination. For example, women may be expected to prioritize domestic responsibilities over their careers, which can impact their advancement.

Reproductive rights


Women's reproductive rights, including access to contraception and safe abortion, are often limited or restricted, particularly in some countries. This can have a significant impact on their health and well-being.
